ECHIDNAS


Echidnas are commonly referred to as spiny anteaters as the two are closely related. Echidnas, along with Platypuses, are the only mammals to lay eggs.

Unlike the Platypus, the Echidna has adapted to live entirely on land, and is covered in tiny spikes.

What do Echidnas do?

Found mostly in Australia and New Zealand, Echidnas are solitary animals with no teeth, so they spend their time digging through dirt and eating worms.

Did you know?

Echidnas are named after the Greek monster Echidna, who was a half-woman, half-snake, as the animal was perceived to have of both mammals and animals.

But don't worry! These creatures only weigh around 7-20lbs!
